The composition of the groups is identical to Part 1.

At the beginning of this part, the computer program will randomly select one group among those present in the room. This group will be referred to as the Passive group. The other groups will be referred to as the Active groups.

The final gains of the members of the Passive group will depend on their Part 1 gains as well as the decisions made by the members of the Active groups in Part 2.

Since the members of the Passive group will not make any decisions in this part, the instructions below concern only the members of the Active groups.
Once all participants have read these instructions, you will be informed whether you are in the Passive group or in an Active group.

Roles and decisions in the Active groups

In each Active group, the computer program randomly assigns a role to each of the {{ PLAYERS_PER_GROUP }} players. There will be one Red Player and two Blue Players.

The Red Player can increase his/her earnings by “stealing” ECU from the Passive group. Concretely, the Red Player must make a binary decision: to steal or not to steal ECU from the Passive group.

  • If the Red Player decides to steal ECU, he/she gains {{ STEALING_AMOUNT }} ECU, while the Passive group loses {{ STEALING_LOSS }} ECU in total: each member of the Passive group loses {{ STEALING_LOSS_INDIV }} ECU ({{ STEALING_LOSS_INDIV }} ECU × {{ PLAYERS_PER_GROUP }} = {{ STEALING_LOSS }} ECU).
  • If the Red Player decides not to steal ECU from the Passive group, all participants’ earnings remain unchanged compared to those of Part 1.

A Red Player who decides to steal ECU from the Passive group is liable to incur a penalty of {{ STEALING_PENALTY }} ECU if reported by a Blue Player.

Reporting rules

In each Active group, the two Blue Players must decide whether or not to report the Red Player of their group if he/she has stolen ECU from the Passive group. Reporting is anonymous.

  • If a Blue Player reports the Red Player and the Red Player has stolen:
    The Red Player has a probability of {{ AUDIT_PROBABILITY_STR }} of being penalized, and a probability of {{ NON_AUDIT_PROBABILITY_STR }} of not being penalized. {{ if subsession.reward }}
    • If the Red Player is penalized, the Blue Player pays a reporting cost of {{ REPORTING_COST }} ECU and receives a reward of {{ REPORTING_REWARD }} ECU.
    • If the Red Player is not penalized, the Blue Player pays no cost and receives no reward.
    {{ else }}
    • If the Red Player is penalized, the Blue Player pays a reporting cost of {{ REPORTING_COST }} ECU.
    • If the Red Player is not penalized, the Blue Player pays no cost.
    {{ endif }}
  • If a Blue Player reports the Red Player but the Red Player did not steal:
    {{ if subsession.reward }} The Red Player incurs no penalty and the Blue Player incurs no reporting cost (and receives no reward). {{ else }} The Red Player incurs no penalty and the Blue Player incurs no reporting cost. {{ endif }}
Randomized reporting

Once both Blue Players have made their decisions, the computer randomly selects one of the two Blue Players’ decisions (to report or not). If the selected decision is to report, the Red Player is reported; otherwise, nothing happens.

Summary of key points

One group will be randomly selected as the Passive group. The other groups will be Active.
In each Active group:

  • One participant is the Red Player and two participants are the Blue Players.
  • The Red Player decides whether or not to steal ECU from the Passive group (stealing adds {{ STEALING_AMOUNT }} ECU to his/her earnings and removes {{ STEALING_LOSS }} ECU from the Passive group).
  • The Blue Players decide whether or not to report the Red Player if he/she steals:
    • The program randomly selects the decision of one of the two Blue Players.
    • If the selected decision is to report and the Red Player has stolen, then the Red Player incurs a penalty of {{ STEALING_PENALTY }} ECU with a probability of {{ AUDIT_PROBABILITY_STR }}, and no penalty with a probability of {{ NON_AUDIT_PROBABILITY_STR }}. {{ if subsession.reward }} If the Red Player is penalized, the Blue Player pays the reporting cost of {{ REPORTING_COST }} ECU and receives a reward of {{ REPORTING_REWARD }} ECU. {{ else }} If the Red Player is penalized, the Blue Player pays the reporting cost of {{ REPORTING_COST }} ECU. {{ endif }}
    • If a Blue Player reports the Red Player but the latter has not stolen, then neither player incurs any consequence {{ if subsession.reward }} (no penalty, no cost, no reward). {{ else }} (no penalty, no cost). {{ endif }}
